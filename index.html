— How it works

How does paying with your face work?

Verix binds a shopper's payment credential to a face template during a one-time enrollment. At checkout, the terminal captures a live face scan, confirms liveness, and matches it to the credential in under 400 ms — then authorizes the charge with cryptographic, biometric proof the real account holder approved it.

  1. 01

    Enroll once

    The shopper links a card or bank account to their face in the app or at a kiosk. Verix keeps a one-way face template — never a photo or card number.

  2. 02

    Verify at checkout

    The terminal scans the shopper's face, checks liveness to block photos and masks, and matches the template in under 400 ms.

  3. 03

    Authorize with proof

    Verix authorizes the charge and attaches signed biometric proof of consent — the record that defeats chargeback fraud.

  4. 04

    Settle and reconcile

    Funds settle through your existing processor. Any dispute arrives with the proof already attached.

— FAQ

Questions operators ask.

How does Verix stop chargeback fraud?

Every Verix payment includes a signed biometric proof that the real account holder was physically present and approved the charge. When a friendly-fraud dispute comes in, that proof is attached to the transaction — so issuers reverse far fewer of them.

What happens to card data?

There isn't any at the counter. Verix replaces the card with a face-bound credential and stores a one-way biometric template, not photos or card numbers. A breach of the terminal yields nothing usable.

Can someone spoof a face to pay?

Verix runs presentation-attack detection (liveness) tested to ISO/IEC 30107 and matches against NIST-evaluated models, with a false-accept rate near 1 in 1,000,000. Photos, masks, and screens are rejected.

How fast is checkout?

A face scan verifies in under 400 milliseconds — faster than a chip insert. There's no tap, PIN, or phone to fumble with.

Do shoppers have to enroll?

Yes, once. Shoppers link a card or bank account to their face in the Verix app or at an in-store kiosk. After that, they pay by looking at the terminal.

Is biometric data private and compliant?

Verix is built for GDPR and U.S. biometric laws like Illinois BIPA: explicit consent, one-way templates, no raw images retained, and shopper-controlled deletion.
